How does ranked-choice voting actually work?

psychology

Ranked-choice voting (RCV) allows voters to rank multiple candidates in order of preference rather than selecting just one.

If a candidate wins a majority of first-preference votes, they win. If not, the candidate with the fewest first-preference votes is eliminated, and their votes are redistributed to the voters' second choices. This process continues until a candidate achieves a majority.
